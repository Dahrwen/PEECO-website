Tropical Rainforests

The Philippines' dense rainforests stretch across Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao teeming with dipterocarp trees that form a towering multi-layered canopy sheltering thousands of species.

Montane Forests

At elevations above 1,000 meters, mossy cloud forests cling to mountain peaks like Mt. Apo and Mt. Pulag critical watersheds that supply freshwater to millions of Filipinos below.

Mangrove Ecosystems

Coastal mangroves act as nurseries for marine life and natural barriers against storm surges. The Philippines once had the world's most diverse mangrove forests many now under threat.

Breathing Life Into Our Air

A single mature tree produces roughly 100 kilograms of oxygen per year, enough to sustain 2 to 3 people for an entire year. Philippine dipterocarp forests, with their massive canopy cover, produce millions of tons of oxygen annually.

22 kg
CO₂ absorbed per year

Fighting Climate Change Daily

Each tree absorbs around 22 kilograms of carbon dioxide annually. Old-growth tropical trees like those in the Sierra Madre range can store over a ton of carbon over their lifetime, making Philippine forests critical carbon sinks.

The Philippines Has Lost Over 90% of Its Original Forest Cover

From 21 million hectares of primary forest in the early 1900s, only about 1.8 million hectares of old-growth forest remain today. Deforestation costs the Philippines roughly 47 million tons of oxygen production and enormous CO₂ sequestration capacity each year.

Forest Wildlife at Risk

Faces of Extinction

These animals call the Philippine forest home. As deforestation accelerates, they lose everything — habitat, food, shelter, and survival.

🦅
Critically Endangered

Philippine Eagle

Pithecophaga jefferyi

The world's largest eagle and the Philippines' national bird. Fewer than 800 remain in the wild. Each pair needs up to 130 km² of old-growth forest to survive — forest that is rapidly disappearing.

Primary Threat
🪓 Habitat Loss from Logging
🦌
Critically Endangered

Visayan Spotted Deer

Rusa alfredi

One of the world's rarest deer, endemic to the Visayan islands. Its lowland and upland forest habitat has been reduced to tiny fragments by agriculture and illegal logging, leaving isolated, vulnerable populations.

Primary Threat
🌾 Deforestation for Agriculture
🐒
Endangered

Philippine Tarsier

Carlito syrichta

One of the world's smallest primates, found only in Bohol, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao. Extraordinarily sensitive to light and noise, these tiny creatures die of stress when their forest homes are disturbed or destroyed.

Primary Threat
🏗️ Illegal Wildlife Trade & Habitat Loss
🐊
Critically Endangered

Philippine Crocodile

Crocodylus mindorensis

One of the world's most endangered crocodiles, with only 100–250 individuals estimated to survive in the wild. Freshwater forest rivers and wetlands are their home — increasingly drained and converted.

Primary Threat
🌊 Wetland Drainage & Hunting
🦋
Vulnerable

Luzon Peacock Swallowtail

Papilio chikae

Among the most spectacular butterflies in Asia, this species is endemic to Luzon's montane forests above 1,500m. Its narrow habitat range makes it acutely vulnerable to any forest degradation or climate shifts.

Primary Threat
🔥 Forest Burning & Collection
🦜
Endangered

Blue-naped Parrot

Tanygnathus lucionensis

Once common across Philippine forest edges and coastal woodlands, this brilliant parrot has declined sharply due to the cage bird trade and rapid loss of lowland forest habitat across the archipelago.

Primary Threat
🏚️ Illegal Pet Trade & Deforestation
